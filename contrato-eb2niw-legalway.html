02
Contract Object — Scope of Services

The Service Provider shall provide exclusively administrative support, document preparation, strategic case organization, and operational assistance related to the preparation of an EB-2 National Interest Waiver (NIW) immigration petition, according to the scope, pricing, timelines, and specifications outlined in the approved commercial proposal.

The Service Provider does not provide legal services, legal advice, legal opinions, or legal representation. The commercial proposal and summary table are incorporated into and form an integral part of this Agreement.

Included Services

  • Initial case strategy review and resume/CV evaluation
  • Evidence checklist and organization; review of supporting documentation
  • Guidance regarding recommendation letters
  • Petition package organization and administrative case support
  • Business plan preparation and support with document preparation
  • Shipping expenses; Requests for Evidence (RFEs); case assembly coordination

Excluded Services — Unless specifically agreed in writing, this Agreement does NOT include:

  • USCIS filing fees; Premium Processing fees; Attorney fees
  • Legal representation before USCIS or government agencies; legal advice; legal opinions
  • Publication or media placement services; patent filing services; expert opinion acquisition
  • Credential evaluations; translation services; government fees; appeals; litigation
  • Academic publication production; third-party reports

These expenses remain solely the responsibility of the Client.

Responsibilities of the Parties

2.1 Accuracy of Information

The Client is solely responsible for the accuracy, authenticity, and completeness of all information and documents provided. The Service Provider shall not be responsible for delays, Notices of Intent to Deny (NOIDs), denials, or unfavorable outcomes arising from inaccurate, incomplete, or omitted information. The Service Provider does not independently verify document authenticity.

2.2 Client Cooperation

Case preparation depends substantially on Client cooperation. The Client acknowledges that EB2-NIW petitions are evidence-based and may require academic records, employment history, awards, publications, media exposure, recommendation letters, and supporting evidence.

2.3 Client Inactivity

If the Client remains inactive or fails to provide requested documentation for more than thirty (30) consecutive days, services may be paused. Extended inactivity may affect preparation timelines. Cases inactive for more than ninety (90) days may be administratively archived; reactivation may require additional fees.

2.4 Recommendation Letters

The Client acknowledges that recommendation letters may require multiple revisions and frequently depend on third-party participation. The Service Provider does not guarantee participation, availability, or response times of recommenders.

2.5 Information Updates

The Client agrees to promptly communicate relevant changes including employment changes, new awards, publications, immigration changes, address changes, educational updates, and relevant historical information.

05
EB-2/NIW Specific Terms & Limitation of Liability

4.1 No Approval Guarantee. The Client acknowledges that approval of an EB2-NIW petition is entirely discretionary and determined solely by USCIS. No approval, immigration benefit, or processing timeline is guaranteed. The Service Provider's total liability shall not exceed the amount paid by the Client.

4.2 National Interest Evaluation

The Client understands USCIS may evaluate substantial merit, national importance, ability to advance the proposed endeavor, and benefit to the United States. Final determinations belong exclusively to government authorities.

4.3 Requests for Additional Evidence

USCIS may issue RFEs, NOIDs, or additional documentation requests. Responses to RFEs, NOIDs, motions, appeals, refiling efforts, or additional government requests are not included unless expressly agreed in writing. Additional fees may apply.

4.4 Preparation Timeline Disclaimer

Preparation timelines depend on Client responsiveness, documentation complexity, third-party participation, and outside providers. Specific filing dates are not guaranteed. The Service Provider shall not be responsible for changes in laws, USCIS policies, governmental delays, or events outside its control.

4.5 Scope Changes

Substantial changes in professional profile, immigration strategy, field of work, or case structure may require scope revisions, timeline adjustments, and additional fees.

4.7 Legal Representation Disclaimer

"I AM NOT AN ATTORNEY LICENSED TO PRACTICE LAW AND MAY NOT PROVIDE LEGAL ADVICE OR LEGAL REPRESENTATION." LegalWay Group does not represent clients before USCIS, does not provide legal advice or issue legal opinions, does not act as an attorney, and does not establish an attorney-client relationship. If legal representation becomes necessary, the Client must independently retain licensed legal counsel.
